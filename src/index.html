Research: Measuring Human Emotions Using Computer Vision Through Thermal Imaging

Conference Presentation: International Conference on Information Management and Computer Science (ICIMCIS), 2024

This research, conducted as a two-person project, explores the use of thermal imaging technology for emotion recognition, focusing on the physiological changes that occur during emotional experiences. By using advanced machine learning models such as VGG16 and EfficientNet, the study analyzes both thermal and visual images to identify emotional states like anger, happiness, and sadness. The project achieved significant results, demonstrating the effectiveness of thermal imaging in accurately detecting emotions, overcoming the limitations of facial expression analysis, and providing a more objective and reliable method for emotion recognition. These findings led to the research being accepted for presentation at the International Conference on Information Management and Computer Science (ICIMCIS), 2024.

Key Contributions:

  • Implemented thermal imaging to detect emotional states, addressing the challenge of emotion concealment through facial expressions.
  • Achieved 97.28% accuracy with the VGG16 model and 96.65% with EfficientNet on thermal datasets.
  • The study offers potential applications in fields like mental health, healthcare, and human-computer interaction.

Role:

  • Led the data preprocessing phase, organizing a dataset of over 2500 images for analysis.
  • Developed and trained machine learning models, fine-tuning them for optimal performance in emotion classification.
  • Evaluated model outcomes using precision, recall, F1 score, and accuracy metrics to measure success.

Key Takeaways:

  • Developed strong proficiency in thermal imaging and deep learning model implementation.
  • Demonstrated thermal imaging as a robust tool for emotion detection, opening new possibilities in personalized mental health assessments.
